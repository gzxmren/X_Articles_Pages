I: Pine Script Intro & Requirements

Every time you see an expert trader with a proprietary indicator on their chart (which they're often selling), it was built with Pine Script.

III: Example Prompts & Workflows

The hardest part of vibe coding indicators is thinking about what you want to build and avoiding AI slop in the process.
This section is a dedicated prompt pack to help you get some ideas for what's possible.
Feel free to tweak these prompts and replace components
  1. Divergence Detector
One of the coolest ways to use this vibe-coding system is for spotting market divergences.
Divergences occur when price and momentum tell different stories.
For example, price is making a lower low, but the RSI is starting to make higher lows.
Here's a cool divergence prompt you can use:
I'm building a trading indicator for TradingView. I want you to build it in Pine Script V6 and give me the script to deploy. I want an RSI divergence detector that identifies bullish divergence when price makes a lower low but RSI makes a higher low. Paint a label on the chart that says BULL DIV when this condition is detected. Only fire the signal when RSI is below 50.
2. Multi-Signal Entry
This is essentially a high-conviction filter. Instead of triggering on a single condition like most basic indicators, it only fires when three specific conditions are true simultaneously.
For example, momentum is weak (RSI below 40), the macro trend is bullish (price above 200 EMA), and there's real buying interest behind the move (volume 1.5x above average):
I'm building a trading indicator for TradingView. I want you to build it in Pine Script V6 and give me the script to deploy. I want a buy signal that only fires when all three of these conditions are true at the same time: RSI is below 40, price is above the 200 EMA, and the current candle volume is at least 1.5x the 20 period average volume. Paint a large green arrow below the candle and send an alert when triggered.
3. Liquidation Zone Detector
This is the most crypto-specific indicator in the entire list, and I really only recommend it for crypto pairs.
Here's what it does
In crypto, most retail traders use leverage. When price moves against a leveraged position far enough, the exchange forces a liquidation (a forced close).
These liquidation events can provide valuable insights.
Prompt to use:
I'm building a trading indicator for TradingView. I want you to build it in Pine Script V6 and give me the full script to deploy. I want an indicator that identifies potential liquidation zones by marking the highest high and lowest low of the last 20 candles as horizontal lines on the chart. Color the upper zone red and the lower zone green. Add a label that says LIQ ZONE HIGH at the upper line and LIQ ZONE LOW at the lower line. Extend the lines to the right so they stay visible as new candles form. Update the zones every time a new candle closes.
4. Dominance Signal
Most of you have probably heard of Bitcoin dominance.
Essentially, Bitcoin dominance measures the percentage of the total crypto market cap held by Bitcoin. When dominance is rising, money is flowing into Bitcoin and out of altcoins (risk appetite is contracting)
When dominance is falling, money is rotating out of Bitcoin and into altcoins (risk appetite is growing).
Instead of checking this chart manually, you can just use this prompt (you can even use this on other pairs/asset classes):
I'm building a trading indicator for TradingView. I want you to build it in Pine Script V6 and deploy it to TradingView. I want an indicator that tracks Bitcoin dominance using the BTC.D symbol. Plan, and guide me through how we can execute this.
These are just a few of the workflows you could build, but really, the possibilities are endless.
